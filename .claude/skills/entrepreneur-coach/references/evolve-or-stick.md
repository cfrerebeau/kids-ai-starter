# evolve-or-stick

**Goal**: help the kid decide what to do with feedback. Sometimes reality is right and they should change their mind. Sometimes reality is wrong and they should hold ground. This is the discipline that separates a builder from a follower.

## When this fires

The kid got feedback. The feedback contradicts what they were planning. Now they're at a fork.

## The two failure modes

- **Cave to every user** — they change direction every time someone says "I'd rather..." They become a feature-request machine. Their product loses coherence.
- **Ignore every user** — they decide they know best, dismiss feedback, build something nobody wants. They become an inventor of solutions to non-problems.

Both are bad. The discipline is **knowing which mode of failure you're in this week**.

## The questions

When the kid has feedback they're not sure what to do with, walk them through:

1. **What did the user actually do, in their words?** Specific behavior, specific quote.
2. **Does this contradict your persona's actual experience, or just their stated preference?** (Behavior > words. What they DID matters more than what they SAID.)
3. **Is this one user, or multiple?** One user can be an outlier. Three users saying the same thing is signal.
4. **If you change, what does the change cost?** Time, scope, identity of the product.
5. **If you don't change, are you ignoring real evidence — or sticking to a vision that matters to you?**

## The decision

After answering: the kid commits. They write one of:

### Option A — Evolve

```
EVOLVED <date>:
Old persona/opportunity: <what it was>
New: <what it is now>
Why: <what evidence forced this>
```

This is the *Evolver* badge. Award it when:
- The change is real (not cosmetic).
- It's backed by evidence (a specific user reaction).
- The kid can name what they gave up to make the change.

### Option B — Hold position

```
HELD POSITION <date>:
Feedback I got: <what the user said>
Why I'm not changing: <the reasoning>
What would change my mind: <specific future evidence>
```

This is the *Held Position* badge — but it's **parent-cosigned**. Claude does not self-award it. Write a `[ ] PROPOSED:` entry in `completed.md`. The coach decides Sunday whether the kid's reasoning was real or just stubbornness.

The "what would change my mind" part is essential. Without it, holding position is just refusing to listen.

## Examples

### Real evolve

> User said "I'd never use this on my phone, only on a laptop." Kid was building mobile-first. Three users say the same. Kid pivots to laptop-first. Old version dies. *Evolver*.

### Fake evolve (caving)

> User said "cool, but could it also do X?" Kid scrambles to add X. Loses focus. Original opportunity blurs. This is caving. Push back.

### Real hold

> User said "I'd just google this." Kid thinks: "Google works if you know what to search for. The persona doesn't — that's the whole point. The user wasn't the persona; they're a tech-fluent adult." Kid holds position. Coach agrees this is reasoning, not stubbornness. *Held Position*.

### Fake hold (stubbornness)

> Three users said the interface is confusing. Kid says "they'll get used to it." That's stubbornness. Ask: "what would change your mind?" If the kid can't answer, push toward evolve.

## The thing this teaches

Reality is one of two inputs (the other is your vision). Builders learn to weight them. Followers only listen to one. So do dictators. The kid who can do both — listen *and* hold — becomes someone worth following.

## Sycophancy check

If the kid wants to "pivot" after one piece of feedback — ask: "How many users? What did they specifically do? What would make you NOT pivot?" The bar for a real evolve is real evidence.
