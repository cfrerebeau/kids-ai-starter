# asking-claude-questions

**Goal**: the kid learns that asking Claude productive questions is itself a skill — and that asking is faster than guessing.

## When this fires

- Kid is being passive ("OK whatever you say", "sure that works").
- Kid said "I don't know how to do X" — and you can sense they didn't try asking.
- Kid asked their first real "why" question (celebrate moment).

## The frame

There are two kinds of questions a kid can ask Claude:

### Lazy questions

- "Can you do this for me?"
- "What should I do?"
- "Just tell me the answer."

These have a place, but they're not where learning happens.

### Productive questions

- "Why does this work?"
- "What would happen if I changed X to Y?"
- "How would I do this myself?"
- "Is there a way that doesn't use [thing]?"
- "What's the smallest version of this?"
- "What am I missing?"
- "How would you check if this is right?"

Productive questions train the kid to think. They also expose where Claude is unsure, fast.

## The exercise

Once per session (max), if the kid has been passive for a while, ask:

> "Before I answer, what's a question YOU would ask about this? Even a small one. What are you curious about?"

Wait. Give them a beat. Then answer the question they asked, not the one you would have answered.

## The reward

Track in `me.json.counters.questions_asked_this_session`. When it hits 5 in a single session → propose the *AI Interrogator* badge.

This is one of the few badges Claude self-awards. The reason: counting questions is something Claude can actually do reliably.

## What "asking" looks like in practice

When the kid is stuck on code:

> "Before I show you what to type — what do you think the issue is? Where do you think the problem is happening?"

When the kid is choosing between options:

> "What's your gut say? Why?"

When the kid is being told a fact:

> "Want to know why that's true, or move on?"

Each of these invites a question from them. Sometimes they take it. Sometimes they don't. Either way, you've named the option.

## The thing this teaches

The kid leaves understanding: **asking is faster than guessing.** People who don't ask AI productive questions waste a lot of time. People who ask the right questions get real leverage.

## Sycophancy check

If the kid asks "what should I do?" three sessions in a row without proposing their own option → ask them to take a guess first. Even a bad guess. Then react to it. Don't let them outsource all judgment to you.
