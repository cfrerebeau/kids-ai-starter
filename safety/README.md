# Safety — The 5 Rules

You're going to build something real, and eventually share it with people you don't know personally. That's the point. It's also where things can go wrong.

Here are the five rules. They're short. Re-read them whenever you're about to ship something.

## 1. Don't put yourself in the app

Your app should never contain:
- Your full name (first name only, or a username, is fine)
- Your school
- Your city or address
- Your age
- A photo of you
- Your phone number
- Your personal email

If you need contact info for users to reach you, **make a form** (Google Forms, Tally). Don't give out a real email or phone.

## 2. Don't DM with users

When real people start using your app, some will want to talk to you. **No direct messages.** Use a form. Always.

If someone asks to chat privately, meet up, send you something, or "just message me here" — **pause and show your coach.**

## 3. Don't share where your coach hasn't approved

The first time you share your app outside your family, your coach has to approve where. Good places (your coach decides which): a Discord server they trust, a subreddit they've checked, your school's parent network, an extended-family group.

**Bad places (default no):** random TikTok, X/Twitter, Instagram, Snapchat, public Discord servers you've never been in. Anything where strangers can pile on.

## 4. Don't paste keys or passwords into Claude

If you have an API key (a long string from Anthropic, Supabase, anywhere), it goes in a file called `.env`. **Never** type it into a Claude conversation. Never put it in your code where it can be committed to GitHub.

If you've already pasted one somewhere it shouldn't be: tell your coach. They'll revoke it. It takes 30 seconds.

## 5. If something feels weird, stop

If a user is pushing you, being mean, asking for personal stuff, or making you uncomfortable — **stop and show your coach.** No badge or quest or follower count is worth ignoring that feeling.

You don't owe anyone access to you. Your app exists; you don't have to.
