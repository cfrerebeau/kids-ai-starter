# When Real People Start Using Your App

Eventually a stranger will use your thing. This is the goal. It's also the moment where you need a plan.

## Before the first stranger

- **Coach approves the channel.** Show your coach where you plan to share (which Discord, which subreddit, which group). They check it. They approve once.
- **Your app passes the privacy check.** Re-read `safety/README.md` rule #1. Search your project files for your name, school, city. None of it should be in the app.
- **Feedback comes through a form.** Set up a Google Form or a Tally form with 2–3 questions. The form is how users tell you what they think. Put the form link in the app.

Claude will help you set this up when you get to the *Shipper* badge quest.

## During — the rules

- **All feedback goes through the form.** If a user finds another way to message you, the answer is "thanks, please drop your feedback here: [form link]."
- **You are anonymous-ish.** A first name or a username. Not your full identity.
- **Screenshots before deletion.** If a user says something mean or weird, screenshot it (for your coach), then delete it. Don't argue back.
- **No money.** Don't accept payments. Don't promise rewards. Don't connect to anyone's bank info. If your app might need this someday, that's a coach-and-paperwork conversation, not a kid-decision.

## When to stop and show your coach

Any of these:
- A user asks to meet you in person.
- A user asks to message you on another platform (Snapchat, Discord DMs, WhatsApp, etc.).
- A user asks for personal info (your age, school, city, family).
- A user says or does something that makes you feel weird — even if you can't say why.
- A user is mean in a way that gets in your head.
- You're getting a lot of users and don't know what to do.

Your coach handles it. That's their job.

## The feedback form template

Three questions, no more:

1. Did this work for you? (Yes / No / Sort of)
2. What's the one thing you'd change?
3. Anything else? (optional)

That's it. Simple form = more replies.

## What good looks like

Good is **not** "lots of users." Good is **one stranger you don't know who used your thing, told you something you didn't expect, and you learned from it.**

That's the whole game.
